Our Process.

A project well begun is half done. We give each of our tasks due consideration and time so that your business objective is met. By and large, here’s how we do it:

  • 1. once we receive your request for linguistic services, we analyse the business need, evaluate the project implications and assess its technical and regulatory components;
  • 2. next, we develop a high-level milestone view of the project and discuss the standard planning process by collecting and verifying the source files, reference materials and translation kit;
  • 3. then, we create a dedicated translation memory and opt in for the dedicated expert linguist(s)/subject-matter expert(s) by factoring in the time sensitiveness of your task;
  • 4. once that is done we kick off your project and attentively coordinate the communication and content flow in-between team members; in the meanwhile, we ensure adherence to your approved terminology and style and the proper display of formatted content;
  • 5. afterwards, we perform a keen review of the translated content and coordinate with you for further instructions or stage approvals; by the way, we constantly refer to the assumed quality standard;
  • 6. we subsequently make our preliminary delivery and await for feedback, if needed, on any additional changes to further implement in your project;
  • 7. upon our final delivery, we file the project information for future reference and use.

Our Approach.

We follow the golden mean in both our work and personal life, so even if we incidentally take a shot at an extremely spicy dish we balance that out by a healthy glass of milk. So much so that we also season each one of our projects with an even dose of quality assurance refinement. If you were wondering how we got the hang of it, take a look at the steps that we have come to habitually follow:

  • 1. as soon as we receive your project QA order, we evaluate the content specificity and applicable rules, as well as analyse your instructions and overall complexity of the task;
  • 2. then, we work out the time and resource coordinates and plan our resource reserve based on unanticipated changes;
  • 3. what we do next is get familiarized with the subject matter and the project kit; we touch base on the format of your deliverables and reach a common understanding of the quality requirements with the dedicated PM;
  • 4. as soon as that is covered, we get down to executing the allocated task and resolve any unidentified risks by coordination;
  • 5. finally, we submit our deliverables to you and file the project documentation, recording any and all written feedback on the quality of the translation.
